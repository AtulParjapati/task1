Mahatma Gandhi

Father of Nation

Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, he became a global symbol of nonviolence and peace. Gandhi’s philosophy of nonviolent resistance, or Ahimsa, was central to his approach in combating social and political injustices. He played a crucial role in India’s struggle for independence from British rule, leading major movements such as the Non-Cooperation Movement, the Civil Disobedience Movement, and the Quit India Movement. One of his most iconic protests was the Dandi March in 1930, where he marched 240 miles to challenge the British monopoly on salt, inspiring millions to join the cause.



About The Legend

  • Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, India.
  • Advocated Ahimsa (nonviolence) and Satya (truth) as guiding principles.
  • Led major movements such as the Non-Cooperation Movement, Civil Disobedience Movement, and Quit India Movement.
  • Organized a 240-mile march in 1930 to protest the British salt tax.
  • Fought against the caste system and untouchability.
  • Embraced simplicity, wearing a loincloth and spinning his own clothes on a charkha.
  • Inspired leaders like Martin Luther King Jr., Nelson Mandela, and others in their struggles for justice.
  • Authored "The Story of My Experiments with Truth", sharing his journey and principles.
  • October 2 is celebrated as Gandhi Jayanti in India and the International Day of Nonviolence globally.
